Numbering numbers

In the triangle above, we're going to say that the first row is the one at top, with just a 1 in it. And so we can say things like, "the second number in row 3 is 2," or "the 5th number in row 6 is 5."

Different triangles

The regular triangle starts with a row that just goes like: 1. For all other rows it's like this: The row has one more number than the one above, and each number is the sum of the two numbers directliest above (and slightly to the left and right). Numbers at the beginning and end of a row only has one "parent"-number above, so they'll be equal to just that number (so there's a bunch of ones at the edges of the rows).
