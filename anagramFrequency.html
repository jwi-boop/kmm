Anagram frequency

via GIPHY

Reading a text can be interesting, but you know what's even better? Counting and grouping words anagrams in a text!

How to count?

If you can rearrange the letters in a word and make a new word, the new word is an anagram of the old word. We want to count the words in the text, but if two or more words are anagrams of each other, we will count as if they are the same word.

In the sensible bit of text "oh no, there is no petal on the plate," we have:

One anagram-group with the 2 different words no and on, with a total count of 3 (two no, one on)

One anagram-group with the 2 different petal and plate, with a total count of 2 (one petal, one plate)

We only consider the words appearing in the text we're working with. That is, even though you can make more anagrams from petal than just plate, it's only a group of 2 different words when working with this text. (If leapt had also appeared in the text we would have an anagram-group with 3 different words, and so on.)

What should I do?

Your task is to find the most used (highest total count) anagram-group that consists of 3 different words in the the provided text.
